About

FIRST

FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) was founded in 1989 to inspire youth interest and participation in science and technology. Based in Manchester, NH, the 501(c)(3) non-profit designs accessible, innovative programs that motivate young people to pursue education and career opportunities in science, technology, engineering, and math, while building self-confidence, knowledge, and life skills. FIRST hosts three different competitions based on age group. ​

Lego League

The First Lego League is a robotic competition offered by FIRST Robotics in which children ages 9-14 design, build, and program a robot to solve a real-world problem. Teams are comprised of 2 or more adult coaches and up to 10 team members. The robots are built using LEGO MINDSTORMS® and the competition takes place on a table-top playing field. First Lego League is offered in 80 different countries worldwide and promotes STEM (Science, technology, engineering, and math), problem solving, and team-building.

Tech Challenge

First Tech Challenge is a robotic competition offered by First Robotics for young adults ages 12-18. Following an athletic model, groups of 10+ students design, build, and program a robot to complete a task. The team receives the competition subject in September and competition starts in October and lasts until March.

Robotics Competition

This year's challenge

First Robotics Competition is an international robotics challenge offered by First Robotics for High school Students. Teams, made of around 30 students and mentors, design, build and program a robot from scratch and/or from a kit that FRC sells. The teams have “kickoff day,” where they learn the goals of the upcoming competition and then have six weeks to build the robot for the competition.

Titan Robotics competes in FRC. The video on the left shows this year's competition, which is Steam Punk themed. Our robot will attempt to conquer many of the challenges of this year's competition, including placing gears, shooting high goals, and climbing a rope at the end of each match.
