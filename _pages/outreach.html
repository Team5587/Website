2018 Outreach

Rocketry Camp

Over the summer, Titan Robotics ran a camp for elementary school students to learn about rocketry and the engineering design process. Students brainstormed, designed, built, and launched their rockets and had a great time with their Titan Robotics counselors!

Meeting with Afghan Dreamers Robotics Team

This past October, Titan Robotics met with the Afghan Dreamers to discuss how girls can make an impact in STEM globally. The Titans demonstrated their robot and gave the Dreamers a tour of the shop. They also discussed the importance of a STEM education, especially for women, throughout the world.

NOVA STEM day 2019

For the third year in a row, Titan Robotics worked with students of all ages on the NVCC campus to help them learn engineering skills by building stomp rockets. Students learned about rocketry and the engineering design process during the activity. The team members were also able to demonstrate and teach students about their robot!

Black Girls Code

With the help of two other FIRST teams, Titan Robotics explained the benefits of robotics to students and parents attending the event. We demoed Kernbread and Kernflake, discussed our favorite things about robotics, and encouraged the participants that they have a place in STEM and robotics.

Art on the Avenue

Titan Robotics team members offered so many STEM learning opportunities to the Alexandria community at this event! They enjoyed demonstrating the robot for the kids and letting them each take a turn at driving it themselves. Team members also helped students design their own balloon powered cars and colorful catapults. Little ones even had the opportunity to color robot pictures! It was a fun event for all!
