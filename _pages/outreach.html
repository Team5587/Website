Outreach

Titan Robotics has taken part in many different activities in order to increase awareness of the various FIRST programs and promote STEM in the local community. Our team strongly believes that giving back to the community is an integral part of the FIRST experience, and we do this through the various events that we take part in or help to create. From teaching youth about STEM opportunities to creating educational and hands-on learning experiences, our team is committed to supporting our community.

"Lemonaide Day"

As part of Kids Helping Kids Alexandria's Annual "Lemonaide Day," Titan Robotics hosted a lemonade stand to raise money for local charities. Titan Robotics brought along the 2017 robot, Good Vibes, and informed people about the charities associated with the event, and the team itself. To learn more about Lemonaide Day and the charities associated with it, visit the official Kids Helping Kids Alexandria's website.

NOVA S.T.E.M Day at NVCC

Titan Robotics took part in the 2017 Northern Virginia STEM day at Northern Virginia Community College. At the event, members of the team taught students about circuit design using playdough and taught physics using rubber band powered popsicle stick catapults.

Barnes and Noble Mini Maker Faire

During the 2016 off-season, our team was involved in the second annual Barnes and Noble Mini Maker Faire. At the event, our team displayed the 2016 robot to interested youth and parents. We also provided detailed descriptions of the functions of the robot and information about the team, and answered questions about STEM and FIRST.

FLL Mentorships

Over the 2016 season, Titan Robotics team members helped establish and support the FIRST Lego League Team at William Ramsey School in Alexandria, Virginia. Over the course of the FLL season, team members taught 20 aspiring young students about the basics of programing and building robots.

Women in STEM

Several members of our team are involved with the Women in STEM club, where members are encouraged to pursue education or careers in the STEM. The members of the team involved in this club, among those that are not, actively promote the diversification of our team, and practice the principles of FIRST.

Girls in Technology

Members of our team presented the robot at Women in Science event during the 2016 offseason. At the event, the team taught the students that attended learned about future career paths in the STEM fields and the pathways they should follow if they wish to continue pursuing STEM.

T.C. STEM Night and College Fair

Amid dozens of hands-on exhibits featuring rockets, robots, devices, and more, plus bilingual parent workshops about STEM careers, college admissions, and financial aid, Titan Robotics members gave live demonstrations of their 2016 FRC robot Caligula. Team members spoke with elementary and middle school students and families about how to take part in the various FIRST programs, in addition to sharing their personal experiences with the team.
